Chamber of Commerce

Site Plan Document

1. Site Name

Chamber of Commerce

Reason for the name: The name "Chamber of Commerce" was chosen because it clearly identifies the organization's purpose and mission. It immediately communicates to visitors that this is a business-focused organization that serves the local business community. The name is professional, recognizable, and aligns with international standards for chamber of commerce organizations.

2. Site Purpose

The Chamber of Commerce website serves multiple purposes for the local business community:

  • Business Networking: Provide a platform for local businesses to connect, collaborate, and grow together through events, directories, and member spotlights.
  • Information Hub: Serve as a central resource for business information, local events, weather updates, and community news relevant to business operations.
  • Membership Management: Facilitate the process of joining the chamber, managing membership levels, and accessing member benefits.
  • Community Engagement: Promote local business events, community initiatives, and foster relationships between businesses and the local community.
  • Business Directory: Provide a comprehensive directory of local businesses to help consumers find services and businesses find potential partners.

3. Scenarios

The following scenarios reflect the target audience users and their needs:

  1. Scenario 1: New Business Owner Looking to Join
    Question: "I just opened a new restaurant in the area and want to connect with other local businesses. How can I join the chamber and what benefits will I receive? I'm also interested in attending networking events to promote my business."
  2. Scenario 2: Local Resident Seeking Business Services
    Question: "I need to find a reliable plumber for my home. Can I search the chamber directory to find reputable local businesses? I also want to know about upcoming community events where I can support local businesses."
  3. Scenario 3: Established Business Owner Seeking Growth
    Question: "My company has been a chamber member for 2 years. I want to upgrade my membership level to get more exposure. What are the different membership tiers and how can I showcase my business in the member spotlights section?"
  4. Scenario 4: Event Organizer Looking for Venues
    Question: "I'm planning a business conference and need to find local venues and catering services. Can I use the chamber directory to find these services, and are there any upcoming networking events where I can meet potential vendors?"

4. Color Schema

The following colors have been selected to create a professional, trustworthy, and modern appearance:

Primary Color

#2c3e50 (Dark Blue-Gray)

Usage: Headers, navigation, footer background, primary text elements

Secondary Color

#3498db (Blue)

Usage: Links, buttons, accents, hover states, call-to-action elements

Accent Color

#e74c3c (Red)

Usage: Important alerts, special highlights, membership level indicators

Light Color

#ecf0f1 (Light Gray)

Usage: Background sections, card backgrounds, subtle highlights

Dark Color

#2c3e50 (Dark)

Usage: Footer text, secondary text, borders

Color Rationale:

  • Primary (Dark Blue-Gray): Conveys professionalism, trust, and stability - essential for a business organization.
  • Secondary (Blue): Represents communication, reliability, and technology - perfect for interactive elements.
  • Accent (Red): Creates urgency and draws attention to important information and calls-to-action.
  • Light Gray: Provides clean, neutral backgrounds that don't compete with content.
  • Dark: Ensures excellent readability and maintains professional appearance.

5. Color Schema Use in Document

This site plan document demonstrates the color schema in action:

  • Primary Color (#2c3e50): Used for the header background, main headings (h2), and section titles
  • Secondary Color (#3498db): Used for heading underlines, wireframe borders, and scenario list borders
  • Accent Color (#e74c3c): Used for important highlights and special elements
  • Light Color (#ecf0f1): Used for the main background and wireframe content areas
  • Dark Color (#2c3e50): Used for footer elements and wireframe headers

6. Typography Named and Used

Selected Font: Roboto

Reason for selection: Roboto is a modern, clean, and highly readable font designed by Google. It's professional yet approachable, making it perfect for a business website. It has excellent legibility across different screen sizes and supports multiple weights for visual hierarchy.

Typography Examples:

Light (300): "Welcome to our Chamber of Commerce"
Regular (400): "Connect with local businesses and grow your network"
Medium (500): "Join our community of successful entrepreneurs"
Bold (700): "Chamber of Commerce - Building Stronger Business Communities"

Typography Application:

  • Headers (h1, h2, h3): Bold (700) weight for strong visual hierarchy
  • Body Text: Regular (400) weight for optimal readability
  • Navigation: Medium (500) weight for clear navigation elements
  • Subtle Text: Light (300) weight for secondary information
